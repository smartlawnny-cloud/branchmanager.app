📋 Estimator / Sales Training

5 modules · ~45 min · Site assessment, pricing, closing

1
Site Assessment Walk-Through
10 min
›
▶ Video: How to Assess a Job Site
  1. Arrive on time. Within 5 min of scheduled window. Call if late. First impression matters.
  2. Walk the property with the client. Let THEM show you what they want. Listen first.
  3. Measure DBH (diameter at breast height, 4.5 ft) of every tree being quoted. Use a tape or D-tape.
  4. Identify hazards: Power lines, structures, fences, septic, underground utilities, slopes, access issues.
  5. Assess access: Can the bucket truck get in? Is there room for the chipper? Tight backyard = more labor.
  6. Take photos: Every tree, the access path, any hazards, the overall property.
  7. Note species: Hardwood vs softwood matters for pricing and difficulty.
👀 You're the expert. Clients look to you. Be confident, knowledgeable, and honest. If you don't know something, say "Let me check with our arborist and get back to you."
Field Demo: Shadow Doug on 3 estimates. Then do 2 solo with Doug reviewing your quotes.

Pricing — The DBH Formula
10 min
›
▶ Video: How We Price Tree Work

Base Pricing Guide

Tree Removal$100 per inch DBH (base)
Complexity multiplier1.0x easy → 2.0x hazardous
Bucket truck access-20% if bucket can reach
Crane required+$2,000–5,000
Pruning$50–200/tree (size dependent)
Stump Grinding$150/stump (add-on)
Haul Debris$350 flat
Bucket Truck$75/hr, $300/half, $600/day
Labor$50/hr per crew member
Arborist Letter$250
  1. Measure DBH: 24" oak removal = 24 × $100 = $2,400 base
  2. Complexity: Near house? Power lines? Steep slope? Multiply by 1.2–2.0x
  3. Add-ons: Stump grinding ($150/stump), haul debris ($350), wood splitting ($200)
  4. Round to clean number: $2,400 + $150 + $350 = $2,900. Quote $2,900 or $3,000.
  5. Never price below cost. Use the in-app Job Cost Calculator (BM → Tools → Calculators → Full Rate Sheet) to verify margins.
💰 Our average job is $1,800. Our close rate is 45%. Price fairly but don't leave money on the table. A 24" oak near a house is a $3,000-$4,000 job — not $1,500.
Assessment: Price 5 hypothetical jobs from photos. Doug reviews for accuracy.
3
Creating Quotes in Branch Manager
8 min
›
▶ Video: Branch Manager Quote Builder
  1. Open Branch Manager → Quotes → + New Quote
  2. Select client (or create new from the request)
  3. Add line items: Each service as a separate line — Tree Removal, Stump Grinding, Haul Debris, etc.
  4. Add description: Be specific — "Remove 24" DBH red oak in backyard, near shed, bucket truck access from driveway"
  5. Add photos from your site visit
  6. Set total and review
  7. Send to client — email with approval link, or text the link directly
⚡ Send the quote within 2 hours of the site visit. Our data shows quotes sent same-day close at 52%. Quotes sent 3+ days later close at 28%.
Field Demo: Create and send 3 real quotes in Branch Manager.
4
Follow-Up & Closing
10 min
›
▶ Video: Following Up on Quotes
  1. Day 1: Send the quote (email + text link)
  2. Day 3: If no response — text "Hi [name], just checking if you had any questions about the quote?"
  3. Day 5: Automated follow-up email from Branch Manager (if enabled)
  4. Day 7: Call. "Hi [name], this is [you] from Second Nature Tree. Wanted to follow up on your quote."
  5. Day 10: Final automated follow-up
  6. Day 14: If still no response, mark as stale. Move on.
📞 80% of sales happen between the 3rd and 7th follow-up. Most competitors quit after one email. Persistence (not pushiness) wins jobs.
Assessment: Show your follow-up log for 5 quotes over 2 weeks.
5
Handling Objections
8 min
›
▶ Video: Common Objections & Responses
  1. "That's too expensive" → "I understand. What we include is full cleanup, stump grinding, and hauling — a lot of companies charge extra for those. What price were you expecting?"
  2. "I need to get other quotes" → "Absolutely, that's smart. I'd just mention we're booking 2-3 weeks out right now, so if you decide to go with us, the sooner we know the sooner we can get you on the schedule."
  3. "Can you do it cheaper?" → "I can look at the scope — is there anything we could leave out? For example, if you keep the wood, I can take $200 off." Never just drop price for no reason.
  4. "I'll think about it" → "Sure, no pressure. I'll follow up in a few days. Is there anything specific you're unsure about that I can help with?"
  5. "The other company is cheaper" → "That happens. We're fully insured, licensed in Westchester and Putnam, and have 100 five-star Google reviews. The cheapest tree company is usually the most expensive when something goes wrong."
🎯 Never badmouth competitors. Sell our value: insurance, reviews, cleanup quality, reliability. Let the work speak for itself.
