👷 Crew Lead Training

6 modules · ~60 min · Leadership, planning, client communication

1
Morning Briefing & Job Planning
8 min
›
▶ Video: Running a Morning Briefing
  1. Check Branch Manager app — review today's jobs, addresses, client notes, special instructions.
  2. Brief the crew: At the truck before leaving the yard. What we're doing, where, hazards, who does what.
  3. Equipment check: Verify everything needed is on the truck. Chainsaw fuel, ropes, rigging gear, chipper, PPE.
  4. Assign roles: Who's climbing, who's on ground, who's feeding chipper, who's raking.
  5. Set expectations: Target finish time, break schedule, quality standards.
👷 The morning briefing sets the tone for the entire day. A 5-minute briefing prevents 2 hours of confusion. Never skip it.
Field Demo: Run a morning briefing for a real job. Doug observes.
2
Daily Safety Checklist & Compliance
5 min
›
▶ Video: Completing the Daily Safety Checklist
  1. Complete the checklist at EVERY job site before work begins. Not after — before.
  2. Walk the site — PPE check, hazard ID, drop zone, electrical, barricades.
  3. Document non-compliance. If someone doesn't have PPE, they don't work. No exceptions.
  4. Submit by end of shift for PPE voucher eligibility ($5/week).
  5. Report incidents to Doug within 1 hour. No matter how small.
📋 The checklist protects YOU. If there's an accident and no checklist was completed, the crew lead is personally liable.
Field Demo: Complete a full daily safety checklist on a live job site.
3
Client Communication on Site
10 min
›
▶ Video: Talking to Clients
  1. Introduce yourself when you arrive. "Hi, I'm [name] from Second Nature Tree. We're here for [job]."
  2. Walk the job with the client if they're home. Confirm what's being done. Ask about access, pets, concerns.
  3. During work: Keep noise conversations short. Don't blast music. Be professional.
  4. Scope changes: If the client asks for extra work, say "Let me check with Doug on pricing for that." Never promise free add-ons.
  5. Before leaving: Walk the site with the client. "Does everything look good? Anything we missed?"
  6. Ask for a review: "If you're happy with the work, we'd really appreciate a Google review." Hand them the card.
⭐ You are the face of the company on site. How you talk to clients determines whether they call us again and refer their neighbors. We have 100 five-star reviews because of how we treat people.
Field Demo: Handle a real client interaction on site. Doug or senior crew member observes.
4
Time Tracking & Payroll
8 min
›
▶ Video: Branch Manager Time Tracking
  1. Clock in your crew in Branch Manager at the start of every shift. Make sure everyone is tracked.
  2. Log hours per job — this feeds job costing. We need to know how many hours each job took.
  3. Break tracking: NY requires a 30-min unpaid lunch for shifts over 6 hours.
  4. Overtime: Anything over 40 hours/week is 1.5x. If we're approaching 40, check with Doug first.
  5. Clock out at end of day. Review crew hours weekly for accuracy before payroll.
💰 Accurate time = accurate pay. If hours are wrong, crew doesn't get paid right. That's on you as crew lead.
Field Demo: Track a full day of crew hours in Branch Manager. Show timesheet review.
5
Job Photos & Documentation
5 min
›
▶ Video: Photo Documentation
  1. Before photos: Take 3-4 photos BEFORE starting. Wide shot, close-up of the tree, any pre-existing damage.
  2. During: Action shots for marketing (with crew consent). Interesting rigging, big removals.
  3. After photos: Same angles as before. Show the clean site. These go on Google, website, social.
  4. Upload to Branch Manager on the job detail. Tag the job number.
  5. Damage documentation: If ANY property damage occurs, photograph IMMEDIATELY from multiple angles. Call Doug.
Field Demo: Document a full job with before/during/after photos. Upload to Branch Manager.
6
Crew Management & Conflict
10 min
›
▶ Video: Leading a Crew
  1. Lead by example. Wear your PPE first. Be the hardest worker on site.
  2. Teach, don't yell. If someone does something wrong, show them the right way. Yelling on a job site is unprofessional.
  3. Safety authority: You have the authority to stop ANY work if you see an unsafe condition. Use it.
  4. Conflicts: Handle crew disagreements privately, off to the side. Never in front of clients.
  5. Substance issues: If someone shows up impaired, send them home. Call Doug. Zero tolerance.
  6. End of day debrief: "What went well? What can we do better tomorrow?" 2 minutes.
🤝 A good crew lead makes everyone better. Your team should go home safe, paid correctly, and feeling like they did good work. That's the job.
Assessment: Doug evaluates crew lead performance over first 2 weeks of supervised lead shifts.
