Job Site Setup & Work Zone Safety
8 min
›
▶
Video: Work Zone Setup (upload your video here)
Replace this with a YouTube embed or MP4

Key Steps:

  1. Arrive early. Be at the truck by call time. Check schedule in Branch Manager app.
  2. Set up cones and signs before ANY equipment comes off the truck. 50ft minimum from work area on roads.
  3. Identify hazards — power lines, fences, cars, structures, slopes. Point them out to crew lead.
  4. Clear the drop zone — nothing within 2x the height of what's being cut.
  5. Barricade the area — no pedestrians within 2 tree-lengths. Use caution tape on residential streets.
⚡ Power lines: If ANY branch is within 10 feet of a power line, STOP. Tell the crew lead. We call Con Ed / NYSEG — never attempt it ourselves.

Knowledge Check

1. How far from the work area should cones be placed on a road?
2. What do you do if a branch is within 10 feet of a power line?

Chipper Operation & Safety
10 min
›
▶
Video: Chipper Safety (upload your video)
  1. Pre-check: Walk around chipper. Check blades, feed tray, discharge chute, safety bar.
  2. Feeding: Butt-end first. Stand to the SIDE, never directly behind material. Let the rollers grab — don't push.
  3. Emergency stop: Know where the feed bar reverse is. Hit it if ANYTHING goes wrong.
  4. Never: Reach into the feed tray. Never wear loose clothing, jewelry, or lanyards near the chipper.
  5. After use: Shut down, wait for full stop, inspect blades, clean feed tray.
🚨 The chipper is the most dangerous piece of equipment on the job. More tree worker fatalities involve chippers than chainsaws. Respect it every single time.

Knowledge Check

1. Which end of a branch goes into the chipper first?
2. Where do you stand when feeding the chipper?

Field Demonstration

Brush Dragging & Cleanup Technique
5 min
›
▶
Video: Efficient Brush Handling
  1. Work in the direction of the chipper. Drag toward the feed, not away.
  2. Big stuff first. Drag large limbs to the chipper, then rake small debris.
  3. Protect the property. Don't drag branches across lawns (use tarps). Don't hit cars, fences, flower beds.
  4. Raking: Rake sawdust and small chips. Leave the site cleaner than you found it.
  5. Load wood: Stack logs neatly on truck. Heaviest on bottom. Secure with straps.
💡 Cleanup is what clients notice most. A perfect cut means nothing if the yard looks trashed. Take pride in the cleanup — it's what gets us 5-star reviews.

Field Demonstration

Clean up a real job site. Crew lead evaluates efficiency, property protection, and final result.

Chainsaw Ground Operations
10 min
›
▶
Video: Chainsaw Safety for Ground Crew
  1. PPE check: Chaps ON, face shield DOWN, ear protection IN, gloves ON before starting.
  2. Pre-start: Check chain tension, bar oil, fuel, chain brake. Start on the ground — drop start only.
  3. Bucking: Cut from the top, watch for spring-back on bent logs. Support the log so the bar doesn't pinch.
  4. Carry: Chain brake ON, bar pointed behind you, engine off when walking more than 50 feet.
  5. Kickback zone: Never cut with the tip of the bar. That's the kickback zone.
⚠️ You will NOT use a chainsaw until your crew lead clears you. Minimum 3 supervised sessions before solo ground cuts. No exceptions.

2. What part of the chainsaw bar causes kickback?

Rigging & Rope Handling
8 min
›
▶
Video: Ground Crew Rigging Basics
  1. Communication: Climber says "STAND CLEAR" before any piece comes down. You respond "ALL CLEAR" only when the drop zone is actually clear.
  2. Rope handling: Run the rope through the Port-a-Wrap or lowering device. Keep tension — never let it run free.
  3. Tag lines: Use tag lines on large pieces to guide them away from targets (houses, fences).
  4. Never stand under: Never stand under a piece being lowered. Never stand under the climber.
  5. Rope care: Keep ropes out of the chipper path. Coil and store at end of day.
📢 Communication saves lives. If you can't hear the climber, STOP everything and re-establish voice contact. Hand signals: thumbs up = ready, fist = stop, wave = come here.

Field Demonstration

Operate the Port-a-Wrap for 3 lowering sequences. Demonstrate proper communication protocol.
