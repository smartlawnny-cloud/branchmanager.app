Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

OSHA PPE Training — covers selection, use, maintenance, and employer obligations

Second Nature Tree Required PPE:

  1. Hard hat (Class E) — worn at ALL times on the job site, even during cleanup
  2. Safety glasses / face shield — glasses minimum; face shield when running saw
  3. Hearing protection — ear muffs or plugs when chipper or chainsaw is running
  4. Chainsaw chaps — required when operating any chainsaw, no exceptions
  5. Steel-toe boots — required on all job sites
  6. Gloves — cut-resistant for chainsaw work, leather for rigging
  7. High-vis vest — required when working near roads
🚨 OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132: Employer must provide PPE at no cost, train on proper use, and enforce compliance. Employee who refuses PPE cannot work.

Fall Protection
OSHA REQUIRED
15 min
›

OSHA Fall Protection Training — required for ANY work above 6 feet

  1. Fall protection required at 6 feet (general industry) or 10 feet (construction)
  2. Tree work = always above 6 feet. Climbing saddle + climbing line = your fall protection.
  3. Bucket truck: Full body harness + lanyard attached to bucket anchor point
  4. Inspect gear DAILY — harness, lanyards, D-rings, stitching, hardware
  5. Rescue plan: Every job with aerial work must have a rescue plan. Who goes up? How do we get them down?
📊 Falls are the #1 cause of death in tree care. TCIA data: 45% of tree worker fatalities are from falls. This training isn't optional.

Electrical Hazard Awareness
OSHA REQUIRED
10 min
›

Electrical safety for tree workers — power line distances, recognition, procedures

  1. Minimum approach distance: 10 feet from ANY power line for unqualified workers (that's us)
  2. Only the utility company can work within 10 feet. Call Con Ed (914-681-6500) or NYSEG
  3. Assume ALL lines are energized — even downed lines. NEVER touch them.
  4. If a line falls on equipment: Stay inside. Call 911. If you MUST exit (fire), jump clear — don't step down.
  5. Wet conditions: Electricity travels through wet wood and wet ropes. Extra caution in rain.
⚡ Electrocution is the #2 cause of tree worker deaths (after falls). One touch = death. There is no "just a little shock" with utility lines. 7,200+ volts on residential lines.

Hazard Communication (HazCom / GHS)
OSHA REQUIRED
8 min
›

OSHA Hazard Communication Standard — SDS, labels, chemical safety

  1. Chemicals we use: Bar & chain oil, 2-cycle fuel mix, hydraulic fluid, herbicide (occasional)
  2. Safety Data Sheets (SDS): Binder kept on the truck. Know where it is.
  3. Labels: Never remove labels from containers. Never put chemicals in unmarked containers.
  4. Spills: Contain with absorbent. Report to crew lead. Don't wash into drains or soil.
  5. Fuel handling: No smoking within 20 feet. Use approved containers. Refuel with engine off and cool.
