🧗 Climber / Aerial Operator Training

8 modules · ~90 min total · Prerequisite: Groundsperson training complete

⚠️ Prerequisite: You must complete all 6 Groundsperson modules before starting Climber training. Climbing and aerial work require a solid foundation in ground operations, safety communication, and equipment handling.
1
Climbing Gear Inspection & Setup
10 min
›
▶ Video: Gear Inspection (upload your video)
  1. Saddle inspection: Check all stitching, D-rings, buckles. No fraying, no UV damage.
  2. Climbing line: Run full length through hands. Any hard spots, abrasion, core shots = retire immediately.
  3. Lanyard: Check snap hooks, stitching, adjuster. Must be rated for 5,000 lbs.
  4. Helmet: Class E rated. Chin strap secure. No cracks.
  5. Spurs (if used): Gaffs sharp, pads intact, straps no dry rot. NEVER use spurs on trees being preserved.
🔍 Inspect EVERY time. Gear failure at 60 feet is fatal. If anything looks questionable, don't use it — tell Doug and we replace it.
Field Demo: Inspect a full climbing kit and identify 3 retire/replace items from the test kit.
2
Tie-In Points & Work Positioning
12 min
›
▶ Video: Tie-In Selection
  1. Always tie in above your work. Climbing line must be at or above your position at all times.
  2. Two points of attachment whenever possible — climbing line + lanyard.
  3. Crotch selection: Union (not included) crotches are strongest. Avoid dead wood, cracks, or narrow V-crotches.
  4. Redirect: Use redirect to improve your angle and reduce swing potential.
  5. Never: Never tie into a branch smaller than 4 inches. Never wrap around a limb you're cutting.
⚠️ If you fall, the tie-in is the only thing between you and the ground. Take the extra 30 seconds to find a solid anchor.
Field Demo: Climb to 30 feet, set 3 different tie-in points, demonstrate redirect.
3
Pruning Cuts (ANSI A300 Standards)
10 min
›
▶ Video: Proper Pruning Technique
  1. 3-cut method for branches over 2 inches: undercut → top cut → final cut at collar.
  2. Branch collar: Cut just outside the collar. Never flush cut. Never leave a stub.
  3. Crown reduction: Cut back to a lateral branch at least 1/3 the diameter of the removed branch.
  4. Never remove more than 25% of the live crown in a single season.
  5. Dead wood: Remove dead, dying, diseased, and crossing branches first.
🌳 Bad cuts kill trees slowly. A proper pruning cut heals. A flush cut or stub rots. Our reputation depends on doing it right.
Field Demo: Make 10 pruning cuts on a live tree. Crew lead inspects each cut for collar preservation.
4
Rigging from the Tree
15 min
›
▶ Video: Rigging for Removal
  1. Assess the load: Estimate weight before cutting. Green wood is 2x heavier than you think.
  2. Set the block: Block above the cut. Ensure the block's rated capacity exceeds the load.
  3. Communicate: "SETTING ROPE" → "ROPE SET" → "STAND CLEAR" → ground: "ALL CLEAR" → "CUTTING"
  4. Speed line vs. lower: Speed line for open areas. Lower with Port-a-Wrap near structures.
  5. Notch + back cut: Even on rigged pieces, use a proper notch to control the hinge.
⚡ Shock loading kills equipment and people. Never let a rigged piece free-fall to the end of the rope. Controlled lowering ALWAYS.
Field Demo: Rig and lower 5 pieces from height. Demonstrate communication protocol.
5
Bucket Truck Operation
12 min
›
▶ Video: Bucket Truck Safety
  1. Setup: Level ground, outriggers fully deployed, wheel chocks set. Check load chart.
  2. Pre-flight: Test all controls (boom, rotate, bucket) from the ground BEFORE going up.
  3. Harness: Always wear fall protection in the bucket. Lanyard attached to the anchor point.
  4. Operating radius: Stay within the rated work envelope. Never overreach.
  5. Power lines: Minimum 10 feet from ANY line. If closer, call utility for de-energize.
  6. Emergency: Ground operator must know how to bring the bucket down from ground controls.
🚛 Our bucket truck is a $150,000 asset and a potential death trap if misused. Only trained and authorized operators. Period.
Field Demo: Set up outriggers, perform pre-flight, operate bucket to work position, demonstrate ground emergency lowering.
6
Felling Technique & Notch Cuts
12 min
›
▶ Video: Felling Trees Safely
  1. Plan the fell: Lean direction, escape routes (45° behind), drop zone clear 2x tree height.
  2. Open-face notch: 70° opening. Top cut and bottom cut meet precisely. Hinge controls the fall.
  3. Bore cut for leaners or tight areas — establish hinge first, then release.
  4. Wedges: Use felling wedges to control direction. Never use an axe near the bar.
  5. Call "TIMBER" loud and clear before the final cut. Everyone moves to escape routes.
🌲 A bad fell can't be undone. If you're not 100% sure of the direction, stop. Rig it instead. There's no shame in rigging — only in dropping a tree on a house.
Field Demo: Fell 3 trees with proper notch, hinge, and escape route. Crew lead verifies each.
7
Hazard Tree Assessment
8 min
›
▶ Video: Reading a Hazard Tree
  1. 360° walk-around: Before ANY work, walk the entire tree. Look up, look down, look around.
  2. Signs of failure: Cracks, cavities, fungal conks, dead tops, co-dominant stems, root heave.
  3. Lean assessment: Natural lean vs. structural lean. Hang a plumb line if unsure.
  4. Target assessment: What's the tree going to hit if it fails? Houses, cars, people, power lines.
  5. When in doubt: Call Doug for an arborist assessment. We carry ISA certification for a reason.
Field Demo: Assess 3 trees on a job site. Identify hazards, recommend approach.
8
Emergency Rescue (Aerial)
10 min
›
▶ Video: Aerial Rescue Procedure
  1. Scenario: Climber is injured or unconscious in the tree.
  2. Call 911 immediately. Don't wait to assess — call first, help second.
  3. Second climber: Trained rescue climber ascends with rescue kit.
  4. Secure the victim: Attach rescue line, stabilize, lower to ground using mechanical advantage.
  5. First aid: Administer first aid on the ground while waiting for EMS.
🏥 Nearest hospital: Hudson Valley Hospital, 1980 Crompond Rd, Cortlandt Manor — 8 min from most job sites. Know the route.
Field Demo: Perform a simulated aerial rescue with mannequin. Must complete in under 15 minutes.
